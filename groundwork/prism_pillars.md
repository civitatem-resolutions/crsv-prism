### 1. Identity Visibility

Measures how prominently a group's identity (e.g., race, religion, caste, gender) is marked or recognized in the conflict context.

*Questions to think about*:
- What does being "marked" or "recognized" mean in the conflict context?
    - Does it mean being targeted? Should a high score mean high levels of victimization?
    - There is no such thing as a perfect victim (ex: The Partition, 1947), how should this be accounted for?
- How can the identity of certain groups be measured?
- What is the basis upon which we choose the identity metric?
    - Potentially historical context, current conflicts, the identity metric with the greatest disparity/prevalence?
- How should the scale be?
    - A purely positive scale, one that gooes from 0 - 10, with 10 being a highly "marked" group?

**Note**: It might be good for more representation of data (like the imperfect victims) if individual scores are derivable after a few simple calculations from a consolidated score.
